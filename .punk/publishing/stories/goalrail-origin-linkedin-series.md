## Narrative premise

AI helped inside the IDE first. The founder could still read diffs, run checks, and decide what to merge.

Then coding agents changed the scale. They started producing larger chunks of work, and manual attention became the bottleneck.

The core question moved from code generation to process trust:

Can a team see that a change corresponds to the goal without reading every line manually?

This led to Goal on Rails: the idea that a goal should be put on rails before it is handed to autonomous execution.

## Commercial subtext

The public story should quietly invite small teams that recognize the problem.

The invitation is not expressed as a CTA. It is expressed as a situation:

- one live repo
- one real workflow
- one team already feeling AI drift
- the chance to influence the defaults while the approach is still being shaped
