# Goalrail trademark and brand policy

The source code and documentation in this repository may be open source, but
the **Goalrail** name, logos, wordmarks, mascot, and other brand identifiers
are separate rights.

## What the open source license covers

Unless a file says otherwise, repository code and documentation that we own are
licensed under the Apache License, Version 2.0.

## What the open source license does not cover

The Apache License does **not** grant rights to use the Goalrail name, logos,
service marks, product names, mascot, or other brand assets except for
reasonable and customary use when describing the origin of the project.

This means you may generally:

- truthfully say that your work is "based on Goalrail";
- link to this repository;
- discuss Goalrail in documentation, benchmarks, or comparison material.

This means you may **not** generally:

- imply endorsement, partnership, certification, or official status;
- use Goalrail branding for a fork, hosted service, package name, website, or
  company identity in a way that is likely to confuse users;
- reuse official logos, visual identity, or mascot art in your own branding
  unless you have written permission.

## Forks and hosted services

Forks are welcome. If you publish a fork or operate a service based on this
code, please use branding that clearly distinguishes your project from the
official Goalrail project.

Good examples:

- "Fork of Goalrail"
- "Goalrail-compatible tooling"
- "Built from the Goalrail open source repository"

Bad examples:

- "Official Goalrail Cloud"
- "Goalrail Enterprise" (unless authorized)
- use of official Goalrail logos on a third-party service

## Reference materials and screenshots

Some repository paths may contain screenshots, reference screens, or other
third-party materials collected for product or design reference. Those assets
may belong to their original owners and are **not** automatically covered by the
project's open source license. If you need to reuse such material, verify the
rights for the specific file first.
